置顶·为什么要开博客

[photo]

我还是自私，生性凉薄，似乎把很多人和事都看得挺淡。

疫情前每次回国多少要找亲朋好友师长叙旧，疫情三年半没回过加上同龄人似乎都已经成家立业，聊也可能话不投机。终于疫情后回国，第一年（2023）基本就谁也没见；第二年（2024）也是先把自己的形成安排了，剩余三天才发朋友圈……

然后我很感动地被饭局轰炸了，当我以为大家都忘了我时。初中班主任风风火火地从另一个县赶回来，找到了当时的语文数学老师，然后喊了一些同学，凑了一桌。见着了我，拉着我的手就往包间里有说有笑地走。以前玩儿得很好的同学，发小，基友又聚了一桌麻辣烫，喝到半夜，然后是我人生第一次找代驾。那晚，我们又短暂地逃回到十几岁的时候。

可能是性格使然，亦或是年龄到了，也可能是搞学术需要长时间专注，现在也不爱事无巨细地发朋友圈了。经过这次回国，感觉还是有必要给关心我的人一些关心我的机会。于是重拾了多年前的写作习惯，开这么个博客。

写什么呢？

中文都手写，然后敲键盘，主要是感性的东西，反思，复盘，感悟等。

英文就直接是键盘敲出来的，主要是纳米医学领域文章的肤浅评论。

翻译由AI完成，我会大致校读。

短期，或者压根儿不会有公开评论区，因为不想分散太多注意力在这上。而且，写字面向的先是我自己，再是其他人，主打一个“真实”。想聊可以私聊。

Pinned · Why Start a Blog
I'm still selfish and naturally detached, seemingly taking many people and things rather lightly.
Before the pandemic, I would always visit relatives, friends, and teachers whenever I returned to China. After not returning for three and a half years during the pandemic, plus the fact that most peers seem to have settled down with families and careers, conversations might not flow as easily anymore. When I finally returned post-pandemic, I barely met anyone in the first year (2023); in the second year (2024), I only announced my presence on WeChat Moments after arranging my own affairs, with just three days left...
Then I was touchingly bombarded with dinner invitations, just when I thought everyone had forgotten about me. My middle school homeroom teacher rushed back from another county, found my former Chinese and Math teachers, and gathered some classmates for a meal. Upon seeing me, she held my hand and walked cheerfully with me to the private room, chatting and laughing. Then I had another gathering over spicy hotpot with close childhood friends and buddies, drinking until midnight - my first time ever using a designated driver service. That night, we briefly escaped back to our teenage years.
Perhaps it's my personality, or maybe it's age, or possibly the need for extended focus in academic work, but I've lost interest in posting every little detail on WeChat Moments. After this trip home, I felt it necessary to give people who care about me some opportunities to stay connected. So I've picked up my old writing habit from years ago and started this blog.
What will I write about?
Chinese content will be handwritten first, then typed, mainly focusing on emotional content, reflections, reviews, and insights.
English content will be typed directly, mainly my superfacial take on nanomedicine research articles.
Translations will be done by AI, with my general proofreading.
In the short term, or perhaps permanently, there won't be a public comments section, as I don't want to disperse too much attention on this. Besides, I write primarily for myself, then for others, emphasizing 'authenticity.' If you want to discuss, you can message me privately.
That's all.

2025Jan 《nonviolent communication》by Marshall Rosenberg

人与人相处难免会出现冲突与摩擦。一走了之，一刀两断，一拍两散，是再简单不过了。但不妨把每次冲突当成进一步了解对方的契机，让自己锻炼和成长的契机，让自己和对方都成为更完善和健全的个体。如是，以后便不会陷入“冲突—决裂”的死循环。

书里具体的东西就不抄了，大音希声。

多去和对方找平衡点，及时同频；

及时抽离冲突本身，站在一个更高的维度上复盘；

多想想如何建立一个可执行的框架，去避免冲突发生和升级；

理解并尊重人的多元化；

多去创造一些美好的回忆来应对将来潜在的一地鸡毛。

Conflicts and friction are inevitable in human relationships. Walking away, making a clean break, or parting ways completely is the easiest solution. However, consider treating each conflict as an opportunity to better understand the other person, as a chance for self-improvement and growth, allowing both yourself and the other person to become more complete and well-rounded individuals. This way, you won't fall into the endless cycle of 'conflict-separation.'

I won't copy the specific details from the book - the greatest truths are often unspoken.

Here are the key points:

Find balance points with others and sync up in a timely manner

Step back from the conflict itself and review it from a higher dimension

Think more about establishing an executable framework to prevent conflicts from occurring and escalating

Understand and respect human diversity

Create beautiful memories to counter potential future difficulties (literally 'a ground full of chicken feathers' - a Chinese expression meaning messy situations)

自由古巴

“Amigo, 你去哈瓦那的行程有托运行李吗？没有的话，我们买一个给古巴人带些物资过去。” Armando在跟我聊质谱之前，总会先聊点别的。

“我回头看看。”

“真的，考虑下。”

我考虑了。但转念想，我真的要干涉别人的生活吗？古巴真的那么匮乏吗？临行前被新老deadline压得喘不过气来，加上经美国去古巴有诸多不确定因素。终究我只是背了个背包。

无序和慢，是这个国家给我的第一印象。

排队入境古巴，我足足站了一个多小时。大厅里挤满了刚下飞机的乘客。没有人组织秩序，有的队列的速度可以是别的队列的五倍快。慢的队伍当然抱怨。后来才知道，如果抱怨，或者抗议有效的话，古巴就不会成为今天这样了。

入住Airbnb后，房东说每天会随机断电大约两三个小时；而哈瓦那以外的城市则是反过来，随机有电两三个小时；使馆区和某些区域是不会断电的。

在第二晚断电的一片漆黑中，Adrian闪闪发光。

Adrian是Airbnb的前台，古巴人，与我同岁，两岁时随父亲在日内瓦呆了两年，后来去了斯洛伐克。教我抽雪茄时真的是一板一眼，煞有介事。我开玩笑说，虽然这是我的第一次，你也别搞得像是要破我处一样。后来断电的几小时中，伴随着朗姆酒，莫吉托，雪茄，听他们聊分手，聊情伤，聊工作中的奇闻，听他对美国和特朗普疯狂输出。

“所有动物都是平等的，只是有的更平等一些罢了。”同理，所有地区都会平等地断电，只是断电的时长不一样罢了。比如，巴拉德罗就会每天稳定断电零小时。两小时车程就可以从哈瓦那到“楚门的世界”，到碧海蓝天，到应有尽有，到灯红酒绿。啥都好，就是假。

全包酒店是国营的，用来招徕游客和外国货币，而员工都是古巴人，领着固定的一个月大约25美金的工资。而酒店外一墙之隔，开复古老爷车的“个体户”就像狼在等溜出猪圈的猪一样，十分钟比墙内员工十天还赚得多。可能他们懂了“造导弹的不如卖茶叶蛋的”的道理，有几十年行医经验的医生也会“弃医从…出租车”，毕竟有个“小家”要养。并不是所有医生都要像六十多年前的那位阿根廷医生一样，或者像太平洋彼岸的孙医生和周医生一样心怀“大家”。

许多年前，古巴人是不许入住巴拉德罗的这些酒店的，大约2008年后，每接待20位外国人，便生成一个古巴人的配额。我打心底里恶心巴拉德罗，不排除部分原因是烈日照出了我内心的阴影和孤独。

From a spatial perspective, when you've been to thirty countries, the excitement from the thirty-first country is far less than your first time abroad. Of course, it could also be that I'm just getting old."

I was just practicing meditation and realized how difficult it is to focus. One thought that popped into my mind was something Du Haodong said to me in college. He asked, "Have you ever felt like you've become dumber compared to when you were a child?" I asked, "What do you mean?" He said, "When we were kids, we could read a book for an entire afternoon, but now it's hard to calm our minds down." Ever since I entered university, there have been three major pillars, at least three major pillars: studying/GPA, socializing/community work, research/writing, sports, hanging out with friends, and also sleep. Since then, people have been constantly switching between different roles. It's hard to be like high school, where studying and exams were the only things to worry about, at least that was the case for me in high school. Back then, in a situation where I hadn't even touched a girl's hand, I had to spend an entire summer pretending to understand "Dream of the Red Chamber"; in a situation where I hadn't been in a fight for a long time, I had to pretend to understand "Water Margin," and so on. And I could actually spend a whole afternoon reading "Dream of the Red Chamber" in my grandmother's yard, but now I don't remember anything.

There are too many choices, yet it's difficult to excel at one thing.
