20231219 longer day shorter year

2023-12-19

几天前，在Pieter的圣诞聚会上，跟Kevin聊天，他说他从他爸那儿听来的，when we get old, we feel the dat gets longer but the year gets shorter. 觉得甚有共鸣。我说，这就是diminishing return 吧，从空间的维度上讲，当你去了三十个国家后，第三十一个国家给你的兴奋远不如你第一次出国。当然，也可能是我老了。

刚在练习冥想，发现专注竟然是如此困难。跳入我脑中的一个想法是，大学的时候，杜浩东跟我说，你有没有觉得，自己跟小时候比变笨了。我说，啥意思。他说，小时候看书，一看就是一下午，现在很难静下心来。自从上了大学后，三大支柱，少说三大支柱。学习/GPA，社交/社工，科研/文章，搞体育，耍朋友，还要睡眠。从那时候起，人就在不同角色中转换。很难像高中的时候，就读书考试就完事儿，至少我的高中是这样的。那时候，在女生的手都没摸过的情况下，要花一整个暑假假装读懂红楼梦；在很久都没打过架的情况下，要假装读懂水浒 等等。而我竟然真的能在奶奶家的院子里读一整个下午红楼，当然如今啥也不记得。

选择太多了，却很难吧一件事儿做好。

A few days ago, at Pieter's Christmas party, I was chatting with Kevin. He said he heard from his dad that when we get old, we feel that the day gets longer but the year gets shorter. I found it quite relatable. I said, "Isn't that diminishing returns? From a spatial perspective, when you've been to thirty countries, the excitement from the thirty-first country is far less than your first time abroad. Of course, it could also be that I'm just getting old."

I was just practicing meditation and realized how difficult it is to focus. One thought that popped into my mind was something Du Haodong said to me in college. He asked, "Have you ever felt like you've become dumber compared to when you were a child?" I asked, "What do you mean?" He said, "When we were kids, we could read a book for an entire afternoon, but now it's hard to calm our minds down." Ever since I entered university, there have been three major pillars, at least three major pillars: studying/GPA, socializing/community work, research/writing, sports, hanging out with friends, and also sleep. Since then, people have been constantly switching between different roles. It's hard to be like high school, where studying and exams were the only things to worry about, at least that was the case for me in high school. Back then, in a situation where I hadn't even touched a girl's hand, I had to spend an entire summer pretending to understand "Dream of the Red Chamber"; in a situation where I hadn't been in a fight for a long time, I had to pretend to understand "Water Margin," and so on. And I could actually spend a whole afternoon reading "Dream of the Red Chamber" in my grandmother's yard, but now I don't remember anything.

There are too many choices, yet it's difficult to excel at one thing.
