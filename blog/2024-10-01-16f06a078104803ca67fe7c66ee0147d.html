20241001 Empty blebs

2024-10-01

Things are getting ever complicated…

When encapsulating mRNAs into lipid nanoparticles (LNPs), the key morphological grature is the so-called “bleb”. It’s basically an aqueous compartment where one could expect the hydrophilic mRNAs, well, with some likelihood.

A recent preprint shows blebs when formulating LNPs without any drug.

Some key papers on this matter.

A. Leung et al. (2015) observed blebs when formulating siRNA into LNPs;

Two Mark Brader papers (2020 and 2022) identified blebs as mRNA compartment but showed the possibility of blebs without mRNA in.

Cheng and Leung et al. (2023) elevated ionic strength during formulation to induce blebs.

Thelen et al. (2023) formulate saRNA into LNPs. This single cryoEM image below shows the complexity and heterogeneity of LNPs’ morphologies. (different sizes; mRNA in blebs; empty blebs; co-localization of mRNA bleb and empty bleb within one LNP; various curvatures… )

[photo]

This preprint even produces blebs (of course without mRNA) when formulating LNPs without mRNA.
