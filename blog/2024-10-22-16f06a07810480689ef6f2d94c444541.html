20241022 暹罗听雨

2024-10-22

[photo]

十月正值曼谷的雨季。曼谷下雨是真下，一言不合就下一场瓢泼大雨。白天温度一般在三十度往上，一场有始有终的大雨下到最后决不藕断丝连。一场秋雨一场凉。“及时雨”不过如此。

相比之下，温哥华的雨就太不近人情了。淅淅沥沥，看不到头，且下得比较闷骚，以至于不知道如何应对。

我喜欢听雨看雨。站在青旅的天台上，看着金山寺（Wat SaKet）的不知多少寺庙尽在暴雨中洗礼。

七年前在曼谷遇暴雨，喝了啤酒之后再雨中手舞足蹈；壮年听雨于暹罗的天台，只是静静地看着，仿佛是雨困住了自己。

Siam rain

October is right in the middle of Bangkok’s rainy season. When it rains in Bangkok, it really rains—without warning, a torrential downpour can begin. The temperature during the day is generally above 30°C, and the rain, which starts and ends with purpose, will not stop until it's truly over. After one heavy downpour, the coolness sets in. The "timely rain" doesn’t get more fitting than this.

In contrast, the rain in Vancouver is far less forgiving. It drizzles endlessly, with no end in sight, and it’s rather gloomy in a way that makes you unsure how to deal with it.

I enjoy listening to and watching the rain. Standing on the rooftop of the hostel, I watched the Golden Mount (Wat SaKet) and its countless temples being washed in the torrential rain.

Seven years ago, during a heavy rain in Bangkok, I drank beer and danced in the rain; now, as a middle-aged man, I listen to the rain on a Siam rooftop, quietly watching, as if the rain had trapped me.
