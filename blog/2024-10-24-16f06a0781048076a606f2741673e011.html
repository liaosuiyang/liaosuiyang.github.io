20241024 Bypassing endocytosis

2024-10-24

I first encountered this work where Dr. John Lewis presented at NMIN conference in January 2024. Now it’s published on Cell.

The hightlight is that a kind of transmembrane fusion protein (FAST) is engineered onto their lipidic nanocarrier (PLV) to bypass endocytosis. Well, at least indicated by in-vitro experiments. I would check if cellular uptake (including actively, i.e., endocytosis; or passively, wether it’s fusion or diffusion) can be observed at 4 degree C.

Moving onto in vivo work, the FAST-PLV is not necessarily more potent than benchmark LNP formulation. PLV and LNP were not compared at the same RNA dose due to difference in their tolerability (mouse mortality was observed in their LNP at 1mg/kg). If PLV bypasses endocytosis in vivo, then the actual cytosolic delivery of RNA/DNA can be easily boost by 50-fold.

So, it might be that PLV doesn’t enter cells by fusion under in vivo settings (god knows what protein corona is doing over there) or the cytosolic overloading of exogeneous genetic information overwhelmed the cellular mechanism of translation?

Any other thoughts?
