20241027 佛头

2024-10-27

[photo]

白天看庙，晚上睡觉。庙子总是看不完的，过去还多少有点焦虑，总觉得错过些什么；直到后来明白不完美，遗憾和错过才是人生的常态。就学会了告诉自己：差不多得了。

阿瑜陀耶王朝是泰国历史上最辉煌的王朝，直到百年的泰缅战争被老邻居教做人。赢家总是要操你祖宗十八代的，所以推佛塔，卸佛头。大多佛头都不知所踪，但有一颗被盘根错节的树根紧紧抱住。虔诚的新图和猎奇的游客马哈泰寺拜访这颇有“灵性”的树。或是佛头习惯了高高在上，这次反倒是接了地气了。然而你拍照的时候，会有安保人员监督，确保你是蹲下来的。

树有无灵性我不晓得，倒是大多数的意义都是人赋予的。

Buddha Head

See temples by day, then dream away. The temples are endless; I used to feel somewhat anxious, always thinking I was missing something. But eventually, I came to understand that imperfection, regret, and missed chances are just the norm of life. So, I learned to tell myself: close enough is good enough.

The Ayutthaya Kingdom was the most glorious dynasty in Thailand’s history until, after a century of wars with Burma, the old neighbor taught them some hard lessons. Winners will always desecrate your ancestors, so they toppled the Buddha statues and decapitated the Buddha heads. Most of the heads were lost, but one ended up tightly entwined in the roots of a tree. Devout pilgrims and curious tourists visit this 'spiritual' tree at Wat Mahathat. Perhaps Buddha, accustomed to being high above, has now truly connected with the earth. However, when you take photos, a security guard will make sure you’re kneeling down.

Whether the tree is spiritual, I don’t know, but most meanings are, after all, human-made.
