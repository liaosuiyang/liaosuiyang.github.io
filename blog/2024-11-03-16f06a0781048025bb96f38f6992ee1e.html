20241103 小镇做题家

2024-11-03

期间与青旅结识的新朋友一起去博物馆。谈天说地，比较随意。可能聊到了中国人口的问题，哥们儿来一句：因为有这么多“你们”（中国人），你有时候会觉得自己没那么独特吗？当时我愣了一下，只说你看我名字就很独特啊，每个人的经历都不同啊。最后怼回去说“世界上七十亿人，你有没觉得其实没那么独特呢？”。当然我甚至可以说“你是不是后悔当年德国人没让你更独特”类似的话。不过就有点太狠了。讲完之后，我发现自己是不是有点太较真了。

这些年，也算是见过形形色色的人了。一般的事很难让我破防。但当我发现自己在跟不同的朋友聊这事儿的时候，我不得不承认，我破防了。

五年瑞士，两年加拿大，我几乎忘了自己是个“小镇做题家”。直到这次不必要的曼谷之行狠狠地扇了我一耳光 。在曼谷的两周，的确有一些负面情绪。除了不确定性导致的焦虑外，更多是愤懑。为什么我要遭遇这一出？如果没有这些舒服，我是否可以获得更高成就。那句来自第一世界的“是否觉得不够独特”算是骆驼背上的最后两根稻草。

提利昂·兰尼斯特对琼恩·雪诺说：“让我给你一个建议，私生子。永远不要忘记你是谁，因为这个世界不会让你忘记。将它穿在身上，像盔甲一样，这样它就再也无法用来伤害你。”

感谢曼谷。曼谷再见。

Bastard

While visiting a museum with a new friend from the hostel, we had a casual chat. The conversation somehow drifted to China’s population, and my friend remarked, “With so many of you (Chinese people), do you sometimes feel less unique?” I was momentarily taken aback and just replied, “Well, look at my name—it’s unique! Everyone has different experiences.” In the end, I retorted, “There are 7 billion people in the world; don’t you feel a little less unique too?” I could have even said something sharper, like, “Do you regret that the Germans didn’t make you more unique back then?” But that would’ve been a bit too harsh. After this, I started wondering if I had overreacted.

Over the years, I’ve met various kinds of people, and it’s rare for something to break through my defenses. But when I found myself recounting this experience to different friends, I had to admit—I was affected.

Five years in Switzerland, two in Canada, and I’d almost forgotten that I was once a “small-town exam-taker.” Then came this unnecessary trip to Bangkok, which felt like a sharp slap in the face. During those two weeks, I did feel some negative emotions. Besides the anxiety from uncertainty, there was mostly resentment. Why did I have to go through this? Without these distractions, could I have achieved something greater? That “feeling less unique” remark from the First World was the final straw.

Tyrion Lannister said to Jon Snow, “Let me give you some advice, bastard. Never forget what you are; the rest of the world will not. Wear it like armor, and it can never be used to hurt you.”

Khawp khun khrap and au revoir, Bangkok.
