20241107 AUC fingerprints of LNP

2024-11-07

It’s a delight to see more AUC work on LNP. It’s a love and hate complex for me towards this technique. I love how much information you can get in a single run (such a high resolution of particles’ physicochemical signature) but I hate how steep the learning curve is (for me, was) and how laborious each experiment is. Plus, the AUC community a bit too small. This work led by Dr. Luigi Calzolai use AUC to study mRNA loaded LNPs. No surprise, they float as tiny fat bubbles. The board distribution centered at -50S indicates the heterogeneous nature of LNPs. They assigne the +18S peak to free mRNA. This needs revisit. With over 95% encapsulation efficiency, there shouldn’t be that much free mRNA. Might be a combination of free mRNA and highly loaded LNPs? Not sure why a molar ratio of 50:37.5:10:2.5 (ionizable lipid, cholesterol, DSPC and PEG-lipid) is chosen. It’s very close to “onpattro” but not 1.5% PEG, which can make some difference.
