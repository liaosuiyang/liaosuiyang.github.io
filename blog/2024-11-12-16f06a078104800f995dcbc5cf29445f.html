20241112 RNA multiplexing in LNPs againt HBV

2024-11-12

There’s substantial interest in loading different types of RNA in ONE particle of LNPs, be it gene editing or broad spectrum antiviral RNA drug. 20ish siRNA were designed against a conserved region of HBV while mRNA for IL-2 to activate T cell and B cells. Ideally, one LNP will encapsulate as many types of the 20ish siRNA as possible to be a broad-spectrum antiviral drug. Given the small size of siRNA, my impression is hundreds of copies of siRNA will go into one LNPs via the standard formulation. The target cells for siRNA are the infected (hepatocytes) cells. However, for IL-2, as it’s a secreted cytokine, the requirement for cellular tropism is relatively loose. The IL-2 mRNA may or may not enter with the siRNAs in the same LNP but the therapeutic outcome might not alter too much. The real challenge is multiplexing mRNA (even saRNA) not siRNA.
