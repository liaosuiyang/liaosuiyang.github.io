20250102 一年150篇文章

2025-01-02

新年伊始，学界大佬在推特上晒过去一年的谷歌学术，很自豪一年有多少引用，h-index来到了多少。底下评论的多是称赞，转评论的多是颇有微词。我也是闲得蛋疼去数了下，好家伙，去年一年有将近150篇文章。很难想象他都看过。

相比之下，我导处于当打的耄耋之年竟可以徒手（我记得他不用文献管理软件）揪出我把引用作者的名字拼错。这我是服气的。

期刊，影响因子，引用，h-index，等等，这些无非是学术界的通货。而钱的意义是有效率地调动资源，不是目的。

At the start of the new year, some big shots in academia were showing off their Google Scholar stats on Twitter, proudly displaying their citation counts and h-index scores from the past year. The comments were mostly praise, while the retweets often carried subtle criticisms. Out of curiosity, I counted - good grief, they published nearly 150 papers last year. It's hard to imagine they actually read them all.
In contrast, my advisor, despite being in his elderly years, can somehow manually spot when I misspell an author's name in citations (I remember he doesn't even use reference management software). I am impressed.
Journals, impact factors, citations, h-index, and so on - these are merely the currency of academia. And just like money, they're meant to be tools for efficiently allocating resources, not ends in themselves.
