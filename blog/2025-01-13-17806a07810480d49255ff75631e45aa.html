20250113 颜文字

2025-01-13

加入了UBC的某mentorship program，意在帮助提携下本科迷茫的朋友们，同时也丰富下自己的简历。某学生回我邮件的时候，寄件人并非其姓名，反而是颜文字。我犹豫了一下，还是决定给她指出来。犹豫是因为不想显得自己太多管闲事。在回邮件的时候还特意夸了下她那只颜文字小熊很可爱，算是平衡下“爹味”。

Kaomoji

I joined a UBC mentorship program, aiming to help guide undergraduate students who feel lost, while also enriching my resume. When a student replied to my email, their sender name wasn't their actual name but rather a kaomoji (Japanese emoticon). I hesitated but decided to point this out to her. The hesitation came from not wanting to seem too nosy. In my reply, I made sure to compliment her cute kaomoji bear to balance out the 'parental tone' (literally 'dad flavor' in Chinese, meaning being overly paternalistic).
