2025-02-07

[photo]

Linklater的《爱在三部曲》在温哥华重映，介绍词写得非常动人“Life is made up of small moments, not big ones.” 大学学微积分的时候，郑老师说（大意）微积分学什么，学用心去感受和体会生活的每一个瞬间，每一个微小的点。直到今日，我才理解到“积分”是指将那些瞬间和细节堆积起来，便成为了人生。

Calculus的词源学意义是小石子，而“微积分”更是抓住了全貌。

Life's Integration: On Small Moments

Linklater's 'Before Trilogy' is being re-screened in Vancouver, with a touching introduction: 'Life is made up of small moments, not big ones.' When I was studying calculus in university, Professor Zheng said (in essence) that learning calculus is about learning to feel and experience every moment of life, every tiny point, with your heart. Only today do I understand that 'integration' means accumulating those moments and details to form a life.
The etymology of 'Calculus' comes from 'small stones,' and the Chinese term '微积分' (calculus, literally 'tiny-accumulation-divide') truly captures the whole picture.
