20250221 讲报告，听报告

2025-02-21

讲报告要有”锚点“，可以扔个小笑话，或梗，重置下听众的大脑；要”留白“，给听众思考空间，虽然看似给报告的人在一直输出，实则在大脑层面上，应该是个互动的过程；要”即兴“，不能有讲稿，不能背讲稿，与听众建立连结，展现当下的想法，而不是与自己的幻灯片建立连结。若有意外情况，比如片子卡了，边讲边试着解决（主打一个气定神闲），实在不行再求助。若时间不够，有听众的问题没得到回答，主动在会后去接触。

听报告的时候，随时准备点”简单“的救场问题，不要让别人的报告冷场。若无人鼓掌的话，带头鼓掌，绝对有人跟。若坐前排的话，可拍照发给讲报告的人。

Giving and attending presentations
When giving a presentation, have 'anchor points' - you can throw in a small joke or meme to reset the audience's brain. Leave 'white space' to give the audience room to think. Although it seems like the presenter is constantly outputting information, on a mental level, it should be an interactive process. Be 'spontaneous' - don't use a script or memorize one. Connect with the audience and share your thoughts in the moment rather than connecting with your slides. If unexpected situations occur, such as slides freezing, try to solve it while continuing to present (maintaining composure). Only ask for help if absolutely necessary. If there's not enough time for all audience questions, take the initiative to approach people afterward.
When attending a presentation, always be ready with 'simple' questions to help prevent awkward silences during someone else's presentation. If no one is applauding, take the lead in clapping - others will definitely follow. If sitting in the front row, consider taking photos to share with the presenter.
