2025Jan 《nonviolent communication》by Marshall Rosenberg

人与人相处难免会出现冲突与摩擦。一走了之，一刀两断，一拍两散，是再简单不过了。但不妨把每次冲突当成进一步了解对方的契机，让自己锻炼和成长的契机，让自己和对方都成为更完善和健全的个体。如是，以后便不会陷入“冲突—决裂”的死循环。

书里具体的东西就不抄了，大音希声。

多去和对方找平衡点，及时同频；

及时抽离冲突本身，站在一个更高的维度上复盘；

多想想如何建立一个可执行的框架，去避免冲突发生和升级；

理解并尊重人的多元化；

多去创造一些美好的回忆来应对将来潜在的一地鸡毛。

Conflicts and friction are inevitable in human relationships. Walking away, making a clean break, or parting ways completely is the easiest solution. However, consider treating each conflict as an opportunity to better understand the other person, as a chance for self-improvement and growth, allowing both yourself and the other person to become more complete and well-rounded individuals. This way, you won't fall into the endless cycle of 'conflict-separation.'

I won't copy the specific details from the book - the greatest truths are often unspoken.

Here are the key points:

Find balance points with others and sync up in a timely manner

Step back from the conflict itself and review it from a higher dimension

Think more about establishing an executable framework to prevent conflicts from occurring and escalating

Understand and respect human diversity

Create beautiful memories to counter potential future difficulties (literally 'a ground full of chicken feathers' - a Chinese expression meaning messy situations)
