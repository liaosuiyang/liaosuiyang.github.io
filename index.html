About

Who am I?

I am a scientist currently working in the field of nanomedicine. The work is mostly about developing lipid nanoparticles to deliver RNA as vaccines (to prevent diseases) or as therapeutics (to cure diseases).

Before Vancouver, I would label myself as a materials scientist, trained in a supramolecular chemistry lab where my doctoral thesis investigated nano-bio interactions using single-chain polymeric nanoparticles.

Before Lausanne, back in college, I wanted to contribute to the field of flexible electronics by advancing nanofiber fabrication etc. Before that, I was in engineering mechanics til I found out that I didn’t have much talent in math or much passion for building rockets.

Before Beijing, I was a kid living a linear life connecting two dots: school and home.

The rest is here and now, alive and more…
